# Conceptual core

Rover is a solar- and cellular-powered device deployed toward Pinchard's Island, Newfoundland. It carries imaging and sensing hardware, and it is meant to carry a language model that writes field notes about what the device observes.

The conceptual center is not the chassis. It is **linguistic failure made visible**: a model working from fragmentary language and historical materials — pieces that stand in for broken or incomplete cultural transmission — rather than from a fluent, inherited relation to the place. The notes should accumulate as a living, self-revising record that can contradict earlier entries, misname what it sees, and ask questions instead of claiming finished description.

That framing is why “getting it right” as pure engineering is not enough on its own. Engineering still matters (survival through winter, power, safety), but the artwork’s pressure is on incomplete speech and incomplete belonging.

**Permissions:** specific published sources intended for the training corpus are not cleared for that use in this repository. Public docs describe the *intent* of training on fragments; they do not assert that those rights have been granted, and this repo does not include training text.

**Naming:** the proposed device name is Fielding. Project title remains open. A longer literary justification for the device name is withheld from public materials until permission is confirmed.
