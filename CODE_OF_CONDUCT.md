# Code of conduct

Rover deals with place, language, displacement, and cultural material. Contributors are expected to treat that context with care.

## Expected

- Be precise and respectful in technical and cultural discussion
- Attribute sources; do not present others’ language or stories as freely reusable training data
- Prefer questions and uncertainty over authoritative claims about communities you do not belong to
- Keep consent-sensitive material out of public Issues, Discussions, and PRs

## Unacceptable

- Dumping copyrighted or archival corpora into the repository or tickets
- Sharing personal information about living people without their consent, especially in site- or family-related contexts
- Harassment, bigotry, or extracting “local colour” as spectacle

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close Issues/Discussions/PRs, or block accounts that violate this note. For private concerns, contact the repository owner through GitHub.

This is a short project-specific note, not a full Contributor Covenant. It can be expanded later if the contributor base grows.
